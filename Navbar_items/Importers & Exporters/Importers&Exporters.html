Our Imports


Pakistan's imports are essential for supporting its economy and meeting the needs of various industries. Major imports include industrial machinery, petroleum products like crude oil and diesel, and chemicals used in agriculture and manufacturing. The country also imports food items such as edible oils, tea, and pulses to fulfill domestic demand. Electronics, electrical appliances, raw materials like iron and plastic, and vehicles with auto parts are also key components of imports. These goods help maintain industrial growth, transportation, and technological advancement across the country.

Machinery

Petroleum Products

Pakistan relies heavily on imported petroleum products, including crude oil, petroleum oils, and natural gas, to meet its energy demands.

Pharmaceuticals

Pakistan imports a wide range of pharmaceutical products including medicines, vaccines, and raw materials for drug manufacturing.

Chemicals

Various chemicals are imported for use in industries such as textiles, pharmaceuticals, and fertilizers.

Food and Beverages

Pakistan imports food items like wheat, pulses, and cooking oil to meet domestic demand.

Electronics and Electrical Equipment

The country imports electronic goods, including mobile phones, computers, and electrical machinery.

Raw Materials

Imports of raw materials, such as cotton, are crucial for Pakistan's textile industry.

Vehicles and Auto Parts

Pakistan imports vehicles and auto parts to cater to its growing automotive sector.

Medical Equipment

Pakistan imports advanced medical devices and diagnostic tools to support its healthcare system.









Our Exports


Pakistan’s exports play an important role in the national economy by generating foreign exchange and promoting trade. Key export items include textiles (especially cotton products), rice (notably Basmati), sports goods from Sialkot, leather products, and surgical instruments. Additionally, fruits, vegetables, carpets, and chemicals also contribute to the export sector. These products are recognized globally for their quality, helping Pakistan strengthen its presence in international markets.


_____________________________________________________________________





Textiles

Pakistan is known for its textile exports including cotton yarn, fabrics, garments, and home textiles.

Rice

Basmati and non-basmati rice are among the top agricultural exports from Pakistan.

Sports Goods

Sialkot-made footballs and other sports equipment are exported to international markets.

Leather Products

Pakistan exports leather jackets, shoes, and accessories made from quality leather.

Surgical Instruments

High-quality surgical tools manufactured in Pakistan are exported worldwide.

Fruits and Vegetables

Pakistan exports mangoes, oranges, and various vegetables to Gulf and European markets.

Chemicals

Pakistan exports various chemicals, including industrial and pharmaceutical-grade chemicals.

Carpets and Rugs

Hand-knotted carpets and rugs from Pakistan are popular in international markets.

Handicrafts

Traditional handicrafts like pottery, embroidery, and woodwork represent Pakistan’s rich cultural heritage in export markets.
